Process

Site-specific planning for buildings people notice.

The first step is understanding where the birds are active and why. Photos should show the building face, ledges, signage, roofline, loading area, or balcony zone from a few angles. We look for landing surfaces, sheltered spaces, nesting signs, access limitations, and whether droppings are creating a safety or appearance concern.

From there, we recommend a system that fits the property. Pigeon spikes are often used for narrow landing zones, while bird netting works better for larger openings or covered areas. Cleaning may be recommended before installation if droppings or nesting debris are already present. For operating businesses, scheduling and visibility matter, so we keep the quote practical and easy to communicate.

Materials used

Commercial systems may include stainless or exterior-rated pigeon spikes, UV-stable bird netting, perimeter cable, clips, anchors, tensioning hardware, and surface-appropriate adhesives or fasteners. The material choice depends on exposure, visibility, access, and the type of surface being protected.
