Pet-Safe Balcony Netting

Built for balconies that pets actually use.

Cat and pet balcony netting needs to feel secure, tidy, and livable. We look at the railing, ceiling, side openings, furniture layout, and building rules before recommending a netting approach. The goal is to help reduce risk for pets while still keeping the balcony bright and usable.

Many pet netting projects also solve a bird problem at the same time. A properly planned system can help block pigeons, reduce nesting, and make the balcony more comfortable for the household. If you are unsure what your strata allows, photos are a good first step because they show the balcony type, anchor points, and visibility concerns.

We also consider daily use: doors, furniture, plant placement, sightlines, and how the balcony will look from inside the home. A good pet netting installation should feel like part of the space, not a temporary patch.
