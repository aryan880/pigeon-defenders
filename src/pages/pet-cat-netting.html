Process

Planned around your pet, not just the opening.

The quote starts with photos of the full balcony, railing, ceiling, corners, and any side gaps. It also helps to mention the type of pet, whether the balcony is supervised, and whether birds are already causing a mess. That gives us the context needed to recommend netting that fits the space and the goal.

During installation, the crew focuses on clean anchor points, consistent tension, and closing gaps that matter. Pet netting projects often require more attention to the lower rail area, side returns, and awkward corners because pets may explore places birds do not. If pigeon activity is already present, cleaning or additional bird proofing may be recommended before the netting goes in.

Materials used

Materials may include exterior-rated netting, perimeter support line, clips, anchors, and tensioning hardware. The exact setup depends on the balcony construction and visibility concerns. We explain the practical limitations too, because no balcony system should replace responsible supervision or building rules.

Pet Netting FAQ

Questions about cat balcony netting.

Is balcony netting safe for cats?

Pet safety netting can help reduce balcony risk for cats, but the final setup depends on the balcony, anchor points, pet behaviour, and supervision expectations.

Can pet netting also keep pigeons out?

Yes. A well-planned balcony netting system can support pet safety goals while also blocking pigeons from entering the balcony.

Can you install pet netting on a condo balcony?

Often, yes, when building rules and access allow it. Photos help determine whether the balcony is suitable and what approach will look clean.
