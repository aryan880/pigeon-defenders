Spike Installation Details

Placed where pigeons actually land.

Pigeon spikes are most effective when they cover the exact landing surfaces pigeons use. We review the ledge depth, surface type, height, and nearby nesting activity before recommending spike coverage. The goal is a clean deterrent that prevents roosting without creating a messy or overly visible result.

Spikes are a humane landing deterrent, not a trap. They are often used on signs, beams, narrow ledges, balcony edges, parapets, gutters, and rooflines where birds keep returning. For heavier pigeon pressure, spike installation may be paired with balcony cleaning, netting, or exclusion work to stop the full problem rather than only one perch.

Material choice and spacing matter. We match the spike layout to the surface so birds cannot simply land beside the protected area, and we keep the installation as neat as possible for residential, strata, and commercial properties.

Process

A precise layout matters more than covering everything.

The process begins by identifying the exact landing edge. Photos help us see the depth of the ledge, access height, surface material, nearby nesting spots, and whether the birds are landing, sleeping, or entering a larger sheltered area. We then recommend the amount of spike coverage needed to prevent birds from simply shifting a few inches over.

Before installation, the surface may need basic preparation or cleaning so the adhesive or fasteners can perform properly. During installation, we align the spike strips, protect the underlying surface as much as possible, and keep the finished look organized. For commercial bird control Vancouver projects, we can also plan around entrances, signage, customer visibility, and property manager approval.

Materials used

Spike systems may use UV-stable bases, stainless steel pins, exterior adhesive, mechanical fasteners, or other surface-appropriate hardware. The exact material depends on where the spikes are going and how visible the area is. We avoid one-size-fits-all recommendations because a sign, a concrete ledge, and a metal roofline may need different preparation.

Pigeon Spike FAQ

Common spike installation questions.

Are pigeon spikes humane?

Yes. Pigeon spikes are a landing deterrent. They make ledges uncomfortable for birds to use without trapping or poisoning them.

Where can pigeon spikes be installed?

Spikes can be installed on many ledges, signs, beams, rooflines, parapets, gutters, and narrow balcony edges when the surface is suitable.

Do pigeon spikes work for heavy pigeon pressure?

They work best on landing edges. If birds are nesting in a larger open space, spikes may need to be combined with cleaning, bird netting, or exclusion work.
